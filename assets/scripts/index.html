Letchworth State Park is a gorgeous area in Castile, NY, renowned as the "Grand Canyon of the Eastern US". The park is about 17 miles long, and contains a part of the Genesee River within the trails. The upper and lower waterfalls of the river flow gracefully through the rough stone canyons of the park, as sightseers walk above this magnificent scenery on carefully crafted stone paths.
Established in 1906, Letchworth has been open to the public for over 100 years, and sees over 600,000 visitors a year. Letchworth is a state park that is open year-round, and offers a variety of activities across each season, from swimming in the summer to skiing and snowboarding in the winter.
Letchworth has earned its fame from its famous waterfalls - the upper, lower, and middle falls on which the Genesee River flows. The vast, deep gorge runs through a beautiful length of trails in the park, where a breath-taking view of the scenic atmosphere surrounding the park. Visitors can even take a hot air balloon ride over the area for an aerial view of the falls.
Planning a trip, and looking for more information? Look no further. Consider this site a log of my visits to Letchworth, detailing just a few of the many reasons one might visit such a magnificent trail. Whether you enjoy hiking, biking, kayaking, or just sightseeing, Letchworth offers its serene atmosphere to all.
